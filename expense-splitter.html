Fewest payments, not a web of IOUs

Instead of everyone repaying everyone, the splitter nets out all the balances and matches the biggest debtor to the biggest creditor. A group of six usually settles in three or four transfers, not fifteen.

Not everyone shares everything

The late-night taxi only three people took, the bottle of wine two people skipped — toggle those people off that expense and their share drops to zero while the rest still split it evenly.

Stays on your device

Names and amounts never leave your browser. Nothing is uploaded, nothing is stored on a server — close the tab and it's gone.

About splitting expenses

How does it decide who pays whom?

It computes each person's net balance — what they paid minus their fair share of every expense they were in. People who overpaid are owed money; people who underpaid owe it. It then repeatedly matches the largest debtor to the largest creditor, which yields the smallest number of payments that settles everyone.

Can different expenses be split among different people?

Yes. Every expense has its own participants. Everyone is included by default, but you can tap a person's chip on any expense to leave them out — handy when only part of the group shared a cost.

Does it convert between currencies?

No. Pick one currency at the top and enter every expense in it. If your group spent across currencies, convert each cost to your chosen currency before entering it.

Is my data stored anywhere?

No. Everything runs in your browser. Names and amounts are never sent to any server.
